## The Argument

Three structures have been established as invisible to institutional measurement yet overdetermined by their respective independent frameworks: the suppressed developmental cognitive profile of the high-vantage-sensitivity child; the null threshold state of maximum measurement sensitivity in the vacuum birefringence instrument; and the cooperative social emergence above the critical conditional-cooperator density. Prior documents approached these structures through developmental biology, quantum physics, game theory, and psychology. This document approaches all three from an entirely different direction: **sociology**.

The claim is methodological before it is substantive. Sociology — developed across independent research traditions including field theory, status characteristics research, the sociology of expectations, valuation studies, social closure theory, agnotology, and the sociology of scientific knowledge — has independently converged on a precise account of why real structures remain undetected by institutional measurement. That account was constructed to describe entirely other phenomena: how markets fail to recognize genuine talent, how scientific communities decide which instruments to build, how elite networks reproduce themselves through exclusionary recognition. It arrives at the same architectural diagnosis across all three structures regardless.

The central claim of this document:

**Every detection failure documented across these three structures is, at its core, a failure of what Bourdieu called** ***doxic foreclosure*** **— the organized incapacity of a field's perceptual apparatus to register signals located outside the field's consecrated recognition templates. The signal in each case is located in a state — a null state of minimum competitive noise, minimum evaluative interference, minimum categorical legibility — that the field's recognition apparatus is constitutively unable to reach. The correction is not instrumental. It is categorical. And it is sociology that supplies the vocabulary for why.**

Sociology does not add a seventh framework to an existing overdetermination proof. It identifies the **social infrastructure of the criterion miscalibration** — the field structures, status hierarchies, commensurative practices, and doxic settlements that explain why all three signals are systematically missed, and what structural transformation of the recognizing field would be required to find them.

## I. The Sociological Principle: Doxa, Commensuration, and the Field's Organized Blindness

Pierre Bourdieu's concept of **doxa** — the universe of the undiscussed within a field, the taken-for-granted assumptions so deeply embedded in the field's structure that agents never need to articulate them — is the foundational concept of this analysis (Bourdieu, 1977, 1990). Doxa is not ideology in the sense of false belief. It is the organized incapacity to perceive certain things as possible signals, because the field's categorical apparatus was never extended to the address where those signals reside. The agent within the field does not think that the null state contains no signal; they do not think about the null state at all. It falls outside the universe of the undiscussed. This is what makes doxic foreclosure more consequential than ideological bias: ideological bias can be challenged directly; doxa cannot be challenged because it is never present as a proposition to be contested.

Bourdieu's concept of **illusio** specifies the mechanism of reproduction (Bourdieu, 1993). Agents within a field are invested in the field's stakes — they believe the game is worth playing, that the field's recognized capital forms are worth accumulating. This investment is not conscious; it is inscribed in the habitus. The assessor who reduces a child's capacity to a test score is not consciously destroying information; they are participating in the field's legitimate game in the only form the field makes available. The experimental physicist who does not attempt to measure vacuum birefringence in a terrestrial laboratory is not consciously refusing; they are allocating scarce scientific capital toward investments the field recognizes as credible. The peer who suppresses the outlier's contribution is not consciously sabotaging; they are responding to a payoff structure that the field has made invisible as a payoff structure. Illusio makes the field's foreclosures invisible as foreclosures.

**Commensuration** — the transformation of different qualities into a common metric — is the sociological technology through which doxic foreclosure becomes operational (Espeland & Stevens, 1998). Commensuration does not merely measure; it transforms. When the educational field reduces developmental capacity to a test score, the reduction does not merely fail to capture context-dependent expression; it actively destroys the information that context-dependence was carrying. Espeland and Sauder's study of law school rankings demonstrates that commensuration is reflexive: once institutions are ranked, they restructure their practices to optimize on the metric, destroying the diversity of legitimate performance that pre-commensuration variation had preserved (Espeland & Sauder, 2007). The institutional assessor is not measuring a pre-existing distribution of capacity. They are constituting the distribution through the act of measurement — and constituting it in a form that the commensuration apparatus can reach, which forecloses what the apparatus cannot.

Fligstein and McAdam's **theory of strategic action fields** completes the foundational architecture (Fligstein & McAdam, 2012). Fields are organized around distributions of incumbency: incumbents define the field's rules, legitimate capital forms, and recognition criteria in ways that reproduce their own advantage. Challengers must either adopt the incumbent's terms — accepting the field's categorical settlement — or remain outside the field's recognition apparatus entirely. The three structures analyzed here are all challenger structures: they do not fit the incumbent's categorical settlement, and the field's recognition apparatus was designed, under incumbency, to confirm the settlement rather than detect its exceptions.

## II. The First Structure: The Credentialed Child

### Status Characteristics as Recognition Filters

Joseph Berger, Murray Webster, and Cecilia Ridgeway's **status characteristics theory** is among the most rigorously tested frameworks in contemporary sociology (Berger & Webster, 2006; Ridgeway, 2011; Ridgeway & Correll, 2006). The theory's core finding: in task situations, actors attach performance expectations to status characteristics — both diffuse characteristics (age, gender, race, institutional credential) and specific characteristics (demonstrated task performance) — and use these expectations to filter whose contributions count as signal and whose count as noise. Status characteristics theory is not a theory of prejudice; it is a theory of how status-organized expectation states shape the perceptual apparatus of the recognizing field. The child in the assessment context faces a recognition apparatus organized by status expectations attached to their age, their institutional position relative to certified adults, and their prior credential record. These expectations are not beliefs that the assessor holds consciously. They are the perceptual infrastructure through which the assessor's recognition apparatus operates. High-status actors need less evidence to be recognized as contributing; low-status actors need substantially more. The child whose genuine capacity is context-dependent — present in the default mode state, absent under evaluation pressure — will never produce the concentrated, evaluation-context-optimized evidence sufficient to overcome the status expectation discount.

### The Categorical Imperative and the Illegitimacy Discount

Ezra Zuckerman's analysis of the **categorical imperative** in financial markets establishes a mechanism directly applicable to developmental assessment (Zuckerman, 1999). Securities analysts discount firms that do not fit established categorical templates — not because the firm's fundamentals are weak, but because the categorical incoherence makes the firm's value unreadable by the analysts' established valuation apparatus. Greta Hsu's extension confirms that market actors who span multiple categories pay an illegitimacy discount even when their output quality objectively exceeds categorically coherent competitors (Hsu, 2006). The high-vantage-sensitivity child is categorically incoherent: they perform above expected in unstructured, open-ended contexts and below expected in structured evaluative contexts. The assessor's categorical apparatus — "capable student" defined by consistent evaluative-context performance — has no template for conditional expressiveness. The result is an illegitimacy discount applied not for deficiency but for categorical unreadability. The child is not assessed as capable-but-context-dependent. They are assessed as inconsistent, and inconsistency is not a recognized capacity category.

### Epistemic Injustice as Structural Feature

Miranda Fricker's analysis of **epistemic injustice** identifies two mechanisms directly instantiated in the institutional assessment of the cryptic phenotype (Fricker, 2007). *Testimonial injustice* occurs when a speaker's credibility is deflated due to identity prejudice: the child who reports that they understand but cannot demonstrate understanding under evaluation pressure is not believed, because child testimony about their own cognitive states is systematically discounted by adult institutional actors whose status characteristics theory position gives them epistemic authority. *Hermeneutical injustice* occurs when a gap in the collective interpretive resources puts someone at an unfair disadvantage: the educational field has no shared vocabulary for context-dependent capacity expression, so neither the child nor the assessor has the conceptual resources to name what is happening. Hermeneutical injustice is the sociological specification of doxic foreclosure at the level of available interpretive categories. The assessment is not corrupt. The interpretive apparatus is inadequate. And the inadequacy is socially structured.

### The Matthew Effect in Developmental Recognition

Robert Merton's **Matthew Effect** — the rich get richer, the recognized get more recognized — specifies the compounding dynamics of early recognition failure (Merton, 1968). In developmental sequences, early credentialing events structure subsequent access to developmental environments: the child assessed as average at age seven is tracked into average-track environments that provide less of the precisely the environmental quality on which their expression depends. The recognition deficit is not static. It is a compounding function of the developmental sequence: each assessment cycle that fails to detect genuine capacity forecloses the developmental investment that would have produced the evidence of capacity the next assessment cycle needs. The Matthew Effect transforms a calibration failure into a permanent structural feature of the child's institutional record.

## III. The Second Structure: The Unmeasured Vacuum

### The Sociology of Expectations and Scientific Measurement Agendas

Harro van Lente's foundational analysis of **promising technology** established that futures are not merely anticipated — they are socially constructed through a regime of expectations that shapes present action (van Lente, 1993). Borup and colleagues' synthesis confirms the mechanism: expectations function as sociotechnical drivers, providing direction, legitimation, and resources to technical development; but they also function as filters, directing attention toward anticipated futures and away from measurement programs not yet incorporated into the field's expectational structure (Borup et al., 2006). The experimental physics community's failure to build the instrument for terrestrial vacuum birefringence measurement is not primarily a technical failure. It is an **expectational failure**: the measurement is not yet incorporated into the field's sociotechnical imaginary — the collectively held vision of what measurements are physically achievable, scientifically valuable, and institutionally credible. The eight independent mathematical frameworks that converge on the null balance angle exist in theoretical archives. They have not been translated into the expectational regime that directs experimental resources. The measurement waits not for physics but for sociology: for the expectational shift that makes the instrument thinkable as a fundable program.

Jens Beckert's analysis of **fictional expectations** deepens this account (Beckert, 2016). Economic actors do not calculate expected values; they construct narrative fictions about plausible futures and act on those fictions. The allocation of scientific capital — funding, prestige, graduate student labor — toward specific measurement programs is structured by exactly these narrative fictions. The fiction that vacuum birefringence at terrestrial field strengths is below the threshold of practical measurement has functioned as a self-fulfilling prophesy: because the instrument has not been funded, the measurement has not been made, which confirms the fiction that the measurement is premature. The overdetermination proof disrupts the fiction by providing an independent convergence strong enough to shift the expectational regime — if the sociological infrastructure for expectational revision is present.

### Boundary Work and the Demarcation of Legitimate Measurement

Thomas Gieryn's analysis of **boundary work** — the rhetorical and institutional practices through which experts demarcate legitimate from illegitimate knowledge production — identifies the sociological mechanism through which measurement agendas are policed (Gieryn, 1983, 1999). The physics community's boundary work around experimental feasibility — the informal but highly consequential judgments about what measurements are "real physics" versus "premature speculation" — functions as the field's gatekeeper mechanism for experimental capital allocation. Measurement programs that cross established theoretical paradigm lines without institutional sponsorship from recognized incumbents face a boundary credibility deficit: the measurement is delegitimized not on physical grounds but on social grounds — it lacks the sponsorship of established scientific capital holders whose endorsement would place it inside the boundary of legitimate experimental physics. The overdetermination proof is a boundary-crossing document: it draws on eight mathematical frameworks, not all of which are within the recognized boundary of experimental quantum optics. Its reception will be determined as much by the sociology of the field as by the physics.

### Agnotology: The Social Production of Experimental Silence

Robert Proctor's concept of **agnotology** — the study of the cultural production of ignorance — identifies the active dimension of the field's non-measurement (Proctor, 2008; McGoey, 2012). Ignorance is not the default state from which knowledge departs. It is produced: through funding structures that direct resources away from certain measurement programs; through prestige hierarchies that delegitimize work at field boundaries; through peer review practices that privilege confirmatory over exploratory measurement agendas. The non-measurement of vacuum birefringence at terrestrial field strengths is, from an agnotological perspective, not an absence but an outcome — the product of the field's capital allocation practices, expectational regimes, and boundary work. It is, in Sheila Jasanoff's terminology, **co-produced** with the social order of the experimental physics community: the field's recognition of what counts as a legitimate experimental program is simultaneously the production of what remains unmeasured (Jasanoff, 2004). Agnotology provides the sociological vocabulary for what the overdetermination proof attempts to reverse: a structural recalibration of the field's expectational and boundary apparatus sufficient to make the null balance angle visible as a legitimate measurement target.

## IV. The Third Structure: The Cooperative Remainder

### Social Closure and the Rank-Order Network

Max Weber's analysis of **social closure** — the process through which groups monopolize valued resources through exclusionary definitions of legitimate membership — was extended by Frank Parkin into a systematic sociology of how closure mechanisms reproduce social inequality (Weber, 1922/1978; Parkin, 1979). The rank-order peer network is a social closure apparatus: it defines legitimate contribution in terms — competitive performance, relative rank, comparative advantage — that systematically exclude the outlier's characteristic contribution form. The closure is not enforced by conscious exclusion but by the structure of the payoff architecture itself. When institutional resources flow through rank-order allocation, every actor's rational interest is in maintaining the closure criteria that preserve the current distribution of recognized contribution. The outlier's form of contribution — high absolute output, cross-domain synthesis, unconditional knowledge sharing — is excluded not because it is unrecognized as valuable but because it is recognized as threatening to the closure structure that allocates value. Social closure theory provides the sociological specification of why the sabotage equilibrium is stable: it is stable because the closure mechanism is stable. And closure mechanisms are stable because they are in the interest of everyone inside the boundary to maintain them.

Lauren Rivera's study of **elite recruitment as cultural matching** extends the social closure analysis to the specific mechanism of cognitive profile recognition (Rivera, 2012, 2015). Elite hiring is not a search for the most capable candidates; it is a search for candidates whose cultural signals — institutional affiliation, leisure patterns, interactional style, evaluative-context performance — match the cultural template of the existing incumbents. This is closure at the level of cognitive style: the assessment apparatus is calibrated to recognize profiles that reproduce the incumbents' self-image, and it systematically undervalues profiles whose excellence is expressed in forms the incumbents' cultural template cannot read. The cryptic phenotype and the social closure analysis converge: the outlier's underrecognition is simultaneously an assessment failure (the instrument is miscalibrated) and a closure operation (the template is designed to reproduce incumbency).

### Structural Holes and the Sociology of the Exit

Ronald Burt's analysis of **structural holes** establishes that brokers who span gaps between otherwise disconnected groups have access to information and perspectives unavailable to actors embedded within a single group (Burt, 1992, 2004). The outlier who exits the rank-order peer network to form or join an outlier collective is, in network sociological terms, converting from an embedded position (within a dense, closure-structured network) to a bridging position (spanning multiple intellectual communities with structural gaps between them). The cooperative surplus that the outlier collective generates is not merely a function of its internal cooperation; it is a function of its network position. Because the collective is structurally diverse — assembled through exit from different institutional contexts — its members span structural holes between domains that the rank-order network's internal closure had sealed. The super-additive output is Burt's structural hole benefit realized at the collective level.

### Organizational Ecology and Population-Level Dynamics

Michael Hannan and John Freeman's **population ecology of organizations** established that organizational environments impose selection pressures that favor certain forms and eliminate others (Hannan & Freeman, 1977). Applied to the ecology of cognitive communities, the framework predicts that the rank-order peer network and the outlier collective are two distinct organizational forms competing in the same population-level environment — and that the AI transition constitutes a **niche shift**: a change in the environmental selection regime that alters the fitness gradient between forms. Hannan, Pólos, and Carroll's extension through **organizational code theory** specifies the categorical dimension: organizational forms are evaluated by audiences according to their fit with recognized categorical templates (Hannan, Pólos & Carroll, 2007). The outlier collective, as a novel organizational form without established categorical recognition, faces the founding liability of categorical illegitimacy. Its actual fitness — measured by absolute output per member — exceeds the rank-order network's under the new selection regime. But its recognized legitimacy lags because legitimacy is a sociological phenomenon, not a performance phenomenon. The ecology is changing faster than the categorical apparatus.

## V. The Common Sociological Architecture

Three structures. Three fields. Three detection failures. One sociological architecture. The purpose of this document is to identify that architecture.

**Doxic foreclosure as the primary mechanism.** In each case, the signal is located outside the field's universe of the undiscussed — in the null state that the field's perceptual apparatus was never configured to recognize as a signal address. The educational field's doxa makes productive cognition visible only in expressed, evaluative-context performance. The experimental physics field's doxa makes legitimate measurement visible only in confirmed, paradigm-consistent experimental programs. The organizational field's doxa makes valuable contribution visible only in rank-order-competitive performance output. In each case, the null state — the default mode, the null balance angle, the cooperative surplus — falls outside the doxic settlement as an address where signals could reside. The correction requires **doxic disruption**: the emergence of actors with sufficient field position to make the taken-for-granted visible as taken-for-granted, to bring the null state inside the universe of the discussed.

**Commensurative violence as the operational mechanism.** The field's reduction of complex, context-dependent signals to commensurable metrics is not a neutral technical operation. It is a sociological operation that destroys precisely the information content that makes the null-state signals real. The test score destroys the context-dependence of capacity expression. The publication metric destroys the exploratory value of measurement programs not yet incorporated into the expectational regime. The rank ordering destroys the absolute-output information that would reveal the cooperative surplus. Espeland and Stevens called this **commensurative violence**: the destruction of qualitative complexity through quantitative reduction (Espeland & Stevens, 1998). It is the sociological technology through which doxic foreclosure becomes material.

**Social closure as the reproductive mechanism.** The field's recognition apparatus does not merely fail to detect the null-state signal; it actively reproduces the conditions of its own non-detection. The Matthew Effect compounds early recognition failures. Status characteristics theory predicts that low-status actors' contributions will continue to be discounted absent a structural disruption of the expectation state. Boundary work continuously re-demarcates the frontier of legitimate measurement to exclude programs whose credentials are not yet established. Social closure maintains the exclusionary criteria that prevent outlier profiles from accumulating the institutional capital that would force recognition. These are not independent mechanisms. They are the **reproductive infrastructure** of the doxic settlement: the sociological processes through which the field's categorical apparatus is maintained against the pressure of signals it was not designed to receive.

**Compounding cost of non-recognition.** In each case, the cost of the field's doxic foreclosure is not linear in time. It is multiplicative. Each assessment cycle that misses the cryptic phenotype forecloses the developmental investment that would have produced recognizable evidence in the next cycle. Each year that the vacuum birefringence measurement program remains outside the expectational regime allows the theoretical frameworks to accumulate without empirical test, deepening the gap between theoretical overdetermination and experimental confirmation. Each exit from the rank-order network deepens both the cooperative deficit in the network and the cooperative surplus in the outlier collective, compounding the bifurcation asymptotically.

## VI. Six Independent Sociological Frameworks

Six independent sociological frameworks — developed by different researchers, in different decades, for entirely different empirical domains — converge on the same architectural diagnosis: the structures are real, the signals are in the null state, the field's recognition apparatus is doxically foreclosed, and the correction is categorical before it is technical.

**1. Field Theory (Bourdieu, 1977, 1990, 1993; Fligstein & McAdam, 2012).** Fields are structured spaces of position-taking in which agents compete for consecrated capital forms. The field's structure — its distribution of capital, its doxa, its illusio — determines what counts as signal and what is invisible as noise. All three detection failures are field-structural phenomena: the educational field's capital structure consecrates evaluative-context performance; the experimental physics field's capital structure consecrates confirmatory measurement programs; the organizational field's capital structure consecrates rank-order competitive output. In each case, the null-state signal is located outside the field's consecrated capital forms. It is not low-capital; it is non-capital — an entirely different form of performance that the field's recognition apparatus was never extended to reach. The correction requires field-level disruption sufficient to shift the consecrated capital forms.

**2. Status Characteristics Theory (Berger, Webster, & Zelditch, 1972; Ridgeway, 2011; Ridgeway & Correll, 2006).** Performance expectations attached to status characteristics filter whose contributions count as signal. The mechanism operates in all three domains: the child's age and institutional subordination generate expectation states that discount their performance; the theoretical physicist without established experimental credentials faces status discount in the allocation of experimental resources; the outlier without credential-confirmed status faces contribution discount even when their absolute output exceeds credentialed peers. Status characteristics theory is the sociological mechanism through which the criterion miscalibration becomes a stable, self-reproducing feature of the field rather than a correctable error.

**3. Commensuration and Valuation Studies (Espeland & Stevens, 1998; Fourcade & Healy, 2013; Espeland & Sauder, 2007).** The act of reducing qualities to quantities transforms what is visible and actively destroys the qualitative complexity that makes null-state signals real. Fourcade's concept of **ordinalization** — the sorting of entities into ranked sequences — extends commensuration to identify how ranking produces categorical identities that then organize subsequent recognition (Fourcade, 2016). The child ranked as average is categorically constituted as average in a way that forecloses subsequent recognition of context-dependent excellence. The measurement program ranked below the threshold of experimental feasibility is categorically constituted as premature. The outlier ranked below the payoff threshold is categorically constituted as low-contributor. Ordinalization is the sociological mechanism through which commensurative violence becomes a stable property of the recognition apparatus.

**4. Sociology of Expectations (van Lente, 1993; Borup, Brown, Konrad, & van Lente, 2006; Beckert, 2016).** Expectations function as self-fulfilling social constructs that direct resources, constitute actors, and create the very futures they anticipate. This framework applies with precision to the null balance measurement: the expectation that terrestrial vacuum birefringence measurement is below the threshold of experimental feasibility is not a physical fact. It is a social fact — a collectively held fictional expectation that directs experimental capital away from the measurement and thereby reproduces the expectational regime that called it premature. Beckert's insight is that fictional expectations are not false beliefs; they are performative constructs whose social function is to coordinate action in the face of uncertainty. The overdetermination proof is a mechanism for shifting the expectational regime: it provides independent convergence strong enough to make an alternative fictional expectation — "this measurement is achievable and necessary" — socially available as a coordination device.

**5. Social Closure Theory (Weber, 1922/1978; Parkin, 1979; Murphy, 1988).** Closure operates through exclusionary definitions of legitimate membership that reproduce the existing distribution of recognized capacity. Murphy's extensions specify how professional closure — the monopolization of recognized expertise through credential requirements — operates as a recursive legitimation mechanism: only those already recognized as legitimate experts can certify new experts, sealing the recognition apparatus against profiles that entered the field through non-standard pathways. This is the sociological specification of the outlier's trajectory: the outlier's genuine capacity cannot be certified by an apparatus that only recognizes the standard pathway. And the standard pathway is closed to the outlier precisely because the outlier's profile does not fit the categorical template that the closed pathway was designed to recognize. Social closure is self-sealing: the mechanism of its own reproduction is immune to challenge from within the closed field.

**6. Agnotology (Proctor, 2008; McGoey, 2012; Frickel et al., 2010).** The social production of ignorance is not a residual phenomenon but an active outcome of social processes. Scott Frickel and colleagues' analysis of **undone science** — systematic non-production of scientific knowledge in domains where the knowledge would challenge powerful institutional interests or established paradigms — is directly applicable to the three structures (Frickel et al., 2010). The non-measurement of the cryptic phenotype, the non-measurement of vacuum birefringence at terrestrial field strengths, and the non-recognition of the cooperative surplus are all instances of undone sociology and undone science: domains where the knowledge is theoretically available, where the frameworks demand the investigation, and where the investigation has not been undertaken because the social infrastructure of the relevant field directs attention and resources elsewhere. Agnotology provides the sociological vocabulary for the active dimension of the detection failure: it is not merely that the field cannot see the signal, but that the field's social processes actively maintain the conditions of non-seeing.

## VII. Predictions

The sociological account generates empirical predictions, each independently falsifiable, derived from the sociological frameworks rather than from the prior documentation of the structures.

**S-C1 — Status Expectation Disruption and Recognition Recovery.** Children assessed at or below expected performance in standard institutional contexts, when reassessed by evaluators whose expectation states have been experimentally disrupted — through equal-status contact protocols, competence information provision, or structured interactional conditions that neutralize diffuse status characteristics — will show recognition gains substantially exceeding what regression to the mean predicts. Effect size will be largest for children whose prior institutional record shows the greatest discrepancy between evaluative-context and open-context performance, consistent with the status characteristics theory account of context-dependent signal suppression.

**S-C2 — Commensurative Disruption and Categorical Revision.** Institutional assessors whose commensuration apparatus is experimentally disrupted — through forced qualitative assessment without access to prior quantitative records — will revise their categorical assessments of cryptic-phenotype children at substantially higher rates than assessors with full quantitative record access. The disruption of the commensuration apparatus is the operationalization of doxic disruption at the level of the assessment interaction.

**S-N1 — Expectational Regime Mapping and Measurement Priority.** The time from theoretical overdetermination to experimental priority assignment across measurement programs in frontier physics will be predicted by the sociotechnical imaginary mapping of the relevant field's expectational regime. Programs whose theoretical overdetermination is incorporated into the field's expectational regime — narrated as achievable in the field's prominent review articles, grant priority lists, and institutional programs — will achieve experimental implementation at substantially shorter timescales than equally overdetermined programs outside the expectational regime.

**S-N2 — Boundary Work and Funding Allocation.** The allocation of experimental funding across precision measurement programs will be predicted by boundary work variables — the endorsement of programs by recognized incumbents, the programs' fit with established categorical templates — substantially more strongly than by any direct measure of the programs' theoretical overdetermination or falsification value. This is a direct prediction that the measurement agenda is determined sociologically before it is determined physically, and that overdetermination alone is insufficient to shift the agenda absent sociological disruption of the boundary apparatus.

**S-O1 — Social Closure Dissolution and Output Unmasking.** Groups of high-capacity actors whose institutional records indicate below-expected contribution, when moved from rank-order allocation to absolute-allocation institutional structures, will show output increases substantially exceeding what their rank-order-context records predict. The output increase will be a function of the degree of prior closure — the intensity of the rank-order payoff gradient in the prior setting — consistent with the social closure account of contribution suppression.

**S-O2 — Field Capital Distribution and Recognition Threshold.** The recognition threshold for novel categorical forms — including the outlier collective as an organizational form and the cryptic phenotype as an assessment category — will be predicted by the capital distribution of the recognizing field. Fields with more concentrated capital (higher incumbent advantage, lower challenger access to consecrated capital forms) will show longer recognition lag for novel forms. Fields undergoing capital disruption — through technology transitions, external shocks, or incumbent exit — will show substantially accelerated recognition of previously foreclosed categorical forms.

## The Proof, Compressed

The child at the window is not absent from the field's recognized contribution space. They are in the doxic void — the state that the field's categorical apparatus was never configured to reach because the field's doxa made it unthinkable as a signal address. The assessor's commensuration apparatus reduces the child's performance to a score. The score lives in the evaluation-context distribution. The child's genuine capacity lives in the null-state distribution. The apparatus reads null state as absence. The capacity is there. The categorical settlement is looking elsewhere.

The null balance of the precision instrument has not been measured not because the physics is wrong, but because the measurement program has not been incorporated into the field's expectational regime. The sociotechnical imaginary of the experimental physics community has not yet made the instrument thinkable as a credible, fundable program. The overdetermination proof is not a physics document. It is a mechanism for shifting an expectational regime — for providing convergence strong enough to make an alternative fictional expectation socially available. The physics is not the bottleneck. The sociology is.

The outlier collective has not been assembled because no field yet recognizes it as a legitimate organizational form. Hannan's founding liability of newness applies: the form lacks categorical legitimacy, and categorical legitimacy cannot be accumulated until the form has demonstrated the performance that categorical legitimacy would have made possible. The closure is self-sealing. The disruption is not organizational. It is categorical: the field's classification apparatus must be shifted to make the cooperative organizational form legible as a form before the form can accumulate the recognition it requires.

**The sociological principle:**

Six independent frameworks — field theory, status characteristics theory, commensuration studies, the sociology of expectations, social closure theory, agnotology — developed by different sociologists, in different decades, for entirely different empirical domains, converge on the same diagnosis: the signals are in the null state, the field's recognition apparatus is calibrated elsewhere, and the failure is doxic before it is technical.

Sociology does not require knowing about the biological, physical, or game-theoretic accounts to arrive at this conclusion. It arrives independently. That is the meaning of overdetermination.

The field cannot see what its categories were not built to reach.

Build the categorical apparatus. Disrupt the doxic settlement. Shift the expectational regime.

The structures are waiting.
